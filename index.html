nOPV2 Distribution & Coverage Analysis

Public Health Data Analysis Project | Polio Outbreak Response

Background

Novel Oral Polio Vaccine type 2 (nOPV2) is deployed during outbreak responses to circulating vaccine-derived poliovirus type 2 (cVDPV2). Efficient distribution and utilization are critical to achieving rapid outbreak control.

Objective

  • Assess nOPV2 distribution efficiency
  • Estimate vaccination coverage by district
  • Identify wastage and supply-demand gaps

Data

Simulated district-level campaign data representing operational indicators commonly used in polio outbreak response.

Download dataset

Methods

  • Data cleaning and analysis using Python (pandas)
  • Coverage, utilization, and wastage indicators computed
  • Descriptive and comparative analysis by district and region

Key Findings

  • National coverage exceeded 90%, with sub-national variability
  • Several districts recorded utilization below optimal levels
  • Higher wastage observed in low-utilization districts

Visualizations

Policy Recommendations

  • Risk-based allocation of nOPV2 doses
  • Real-time stock and utilization monitoring
  • Strengthened microplanning in low-performing districts
